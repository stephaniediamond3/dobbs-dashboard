Figure 1
Abortion Rights Status by State Following Dobbs v. Jackson (2022)

Note. States are shaded by their legal status of abortion access as of December 2024. States classified as Restrictive ban abortion prior to fetal viability; states classified as Not Restrictive protect access up to fetal viability (generally defined as 20–24 weeks, varying by state). Specific ban thresholds are displayed on hover in the interactive version. Data derived from state legislative and judicial records.

Figure 2
Post-Dobbs Legislative Changes to Abortion Law by State

Note. States are classified by whether abortion laws were restricted, strengthened, or unchanged following the Dobbs v. Jackson decision. Many states had pre-existing trigger bans — laws written to take effect automatically upon the reversal of Roe v. Wade (1973) — while others enacted new legislation in the years following the ruling. Dates reflect when changes took effect, rounded to the nearest month, as of December 2024. Data derived from state legislative and judicial records.
